How to Convert Images to PDF

  1. Add your images — drag and drop JPG, PNG, or WebP files into the upload area. You can add multiple images to create a multi-page PDF.
  2. Arrange the order — drag images to reorder them. Each image becomes one page in the PDF. The first image in the list becomes page 1.
  3. Choose page settings — select page size (A4 or US Letter), orientation (Portrait, Landscape, or Auto), and margin width.
  4. Click "Generate PDF" — the PDF is created in your browser using jsPDF and downloaded instantly. No file is uploaded to any server.

Why Convert Images to PDF?

PDF is the universal document format — it preserves layout across all devices and operating systems. Common use cases include:

  • Scanned documents: Combine photos of receipts, contracts, or ID cards into a single PDF for email or archiving.
  • Portfolios: Bundle design work or photography into one shareable file.
  • Assignments: Convert handwritten notes or homework photos into a clean PDF for submission.
  • Presentations: Create a simple slide deck from image files.

Unlike other PDF converters that upload your images to a server, TinyImg processes everything in your browser. This is especially important for sensitive documents like contracts, medical records, or ID scans — your files never leave your device.

FAQ

Is there a limit on the number of images?

You can add up to 20 images per batch. For larger sets, split into multiple PDFs. In practice, 50+ images works fine on most devices, but the 20-image limit keeps the UI manageable.

Will the PDF quality be the same as the original images?

Yes. Images are embedded at their original resolution. The PDF does not compress or resize your images unless the page size forces a fit-to-page scaling.

Can I add a password to the PDF?

Not yet. This feature is on our roadmap. For now, use a dedicated PDF encryption tool after generating the file.
